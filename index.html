Insurance Distribution

RBI + IRDAI
Draft norms target effective date: 1 Jul 2026
  • One-click sales banned — banks can no longer sell insurance, credit cards, or other third-party products via single-click approval; separate, explicit consent is now required.
  • Suitability checks mandated — RBI's Feb 2026 statement proposes mandatory suitability assessment before any third-party product (insurance, MF, PMS) is pushed at a branch.
  • Advisory/distribution split — comprehensive RE advertising & sales directions aim to separate the advisory role from the distribution/commission role.
  • NBFCs cleared to distribute insurance — no longer need RBI's prior nod, only IRDAI authorisation, under the Responsible Business Conduct (2nd Amendment) Directions, 2026 (eff. 1 Jan 2027).
  • Bank branding restricted — a bank's name/logo cannot appear on a third-party product document; referral ≠ sale.
  • Bi-annual compliance reporting — REs must file a mis-selling/compliance report every six months.

An LLM-based suitability-check assistant that reads the customer's existing portfolio + stated need, flags mismatched product recommendations before sale, and auto-drafts the disclosure language a frontline agent must show.

Bank Types & Oversight

RBI
Deposit insurance reform effective: 1 Apr 2026
  • Risk-based deposit insurance — replaces the flat 12 paise/₹100 DICGC premium (in place since 1962) with a model tied to bank health.
  • Scheduled commercial banks assessed via supervisory ratings, CAMELS scores, and estimated loss to the insurance fund.
  • RRBs & cooperative banks assessed on available data and risk exposure — a lighter-touch model given data constraints.
  • Vintage incentive — clean-record banks get a premium discount rising ~1%/year up to 25% over time.
  • Local Area Banks & Payments Banks excluded from the risk model; they continue on the flat 12-paise rate.
  • Recovery-agent code unification — RBI is harmonising separate bank/NBFC/HFC recovery norms into one borrower-treatment standard.
Gen AI applied

A call-transcript classifier (LLM + rules) that screens recovery-agent calls for coercive language or unauthorised disclosure to third parties, routing flagged calls into a complaint-governance queue automatically rather than waiting for a customer complaint to surface the issue.

Input: call transcripts / recordingsOutput: risk-tagged escalationControl mapped: fair-practice code, recovery conduct
Sources: RBI Deposit Insurance reform notification (Apr 2026); RBI Feb 2026 regulatory statement on unified recovery agent guidelines.

Fixed Deposits

RBI Master Direction
Master Direction — Interest Rate on Deposits, 2026, eff. 1 Apr 2026
  • Single consolidated rulebook — supersedes the 2016 deposit-interest directives, unifying rules across banks and NBFCs.
  • Minimum tenure floor: 7 days — no FD can be booked below 7 days; shorter needs must go through a savings account.
  • Small-deposit relief — FDs ≤ ₹10,000 can be withdrawn in full within 3 months with no interest forfeited; for larger sums, partial withdrawal up to ₹5 lakh or 50% (whichever is lower) is allowed in the same window without losing interest on the remainder.
  • Mandatory penalty disclosure — premature-withdrawal penalty structure must be disclosed at the time of booking, not after.
  • Non-callable FD exceptions — premature exit is still permitted for bankruptcy, court order, liquidation, or depositor's demise.
  • Maturity-alert mandate — institutions must proactively notify depositors ahead of maturity to prevent silent auto-renewal.
  • TDS unchanged — 10% TDS above ₹40,000 interest/year (₹50,000 for senior citizens) continues.
Gen AI applied

A natural-language FD-terms generator that converts each bank's penalty matrix into a plain-English KFS summary at booking, plus a chatbot trained only on the bank's own disclosed terms — closing the "I didn't know the penalty" complaint category at the root.

Input: FD penalty schedule (structured)Output: plain-language disclosure copyControl mapped: disclosure-gap complaints
Sources: RBI Master Direction – Interest Rate on Deposits, 2026 (1 Apr 2026); RBI FD rule updates effective 1 Jan 2026.

NBFC vs Non-NBFC Classification

Scale-Based Regulation
SBR Amendment Directions effective: 1 Apr 2026
  • Four-layer structure — Base, Middle, Upper, Top Layer, with regulatory intensity rising at each layer; replaced the older NBFC-ND / NBFC-ND-SI / NBFC-D labels from Oct 2022.
  • Base Layer — non-deposit NBFCs with assets < ₹1,000 crore; minimum capital (NOF) raised to ₹10 crore; 90-day NPA recognition applies uniformly.
  • Middle Layer — all deposit-taking NBFCs (any size) + non-deposit NBFCs ≥ ₹1,000 crore; PCA framework applies here and above.
  • Upper Layer — auto-qualifies at ₹1 lakh crore+ assets; also scored on leverage, interconnectedness, complexity; top-10 NBFCs by size always included.
  • New "Unregistered Type I" category (2026) — entities with no public funds and no customer interface, assets < ₹1,000 crore, are now exempt from RBI registration; voluntary deregistration window open till 30 Sep 2026.
  • Non-NBFC distinction — an entity is only an NBFC if lending/investment is its principal business (≥50% of assets and income); operating companies financing only their own customers fall outside this net.
Gen AI applied

A layer-classification assistant that ingests quarterly balance-sheet data and auto-flags an NBFC approaching a layer threshold, giving compliance teams lead time to prep the heavier reporting pack before the regulatory trigger fires.

Input: quarterly financialsOutput: layer-migration early warningControl mapped: proactive regulatory readiness
Sources: RBI (NBFC – SBR) Amendment Directions, 2026; RBI Governor remarks, Apr 2026, on the layer-framework revision.

Home Loans

RBI Directions
Pre-payment Charges Directions, 2025 — eff. 1 Jan 2026
  • No foreclosure charge on floating-rate home loans — applies to all individual borrowers, with or without co-obligants, regardless of fund source.
  • Fixed-rate loans still chargeable — lenders may levy up to ~3% penalty if disclosed upfront in the sanction letter and loan agreement.
  • LTV caps by ticket size — up to 90% LTV for loans ≤ ₹30 lakh, 80% for ₹30–75 lakh, ~75% above that.
  • Repo-linked external benchmarking — floating-rate home loans must reset at least quarterly against an external benchmark.
  • Free balance transfer — floating-rate borrowers can shift lenders without foreclosure cost to chase a better rate.
  • Document return obligation — original property documents must be returned promptly on full closure.
Gen AI applied

An automated KFS-comparison tool that checks a lender's sanction letter against RBI's disclosure checklist (foreclosure clause, LTV, reset frequency) using document extraction + LLM review — catching non-compliant fine print before it reaches a customer complaint.

Input: sanction letter (PDF)Output: clause-by-clause compliance diffControl mapped: disclosure audit
Sources: RBI (Pre-payment Charges on Loans) Directions, 2025, eff. 1 Jan 2026; RBI home loan LTV/benchmarking norms, 2026 update.

Car Loans

RBI Directions
Same Pre-payment Directions framework — eff. 1 Jan 2026
  • Floating-rate car loans — foreclosure/prepayment charges banned, same individual-borrower protection as home loans.
  • Fixed-rate car loans — penalty still permitted; this is the more common structure in auto lending today, so the practical benefit is narrower than for home loans.
  • Lock-in clauses are lender policy, not RBI mandate — a lock-in can override the "no charge" benefit during its window.
  • Mandatory upfront disclosure — penalty calculation method must appear in the Key Facts Statement.
  • No retrospective fee revival — if a charge was waived once, it cannot be re-applied later on the same loan.
Gen AI applied

A KFS-vs-rate-type classifier that flags whether a customer's auto loan is fixed or floating at intake and auto-populates the correct foreclosure-rights notice in the welcome kit — preventing the most common car-loan complaint root cause.

Input: loan rate-type fieldOutput: tailored rights noticeControl mapped: KFS accuracy
Sources: RBI (Pre-payment Charges on Loans) Directions, 2025; RBI circular, 2 Jul 2025, on REs' divergent pre-payment practices.

Two-Wheeler / Bike Loans

RBI Directions
Same Pre-payment Directions framework — eff. 1 Jan 2026
  • Floating-rate two-wheeler loans — covered under the same individual-borrower foreclosure ban as cars and personal loans; most two-wheeler loans are fixed-rate, so coverage is the narrowest of the three vehicle categories.
  • Smaller-ticket, shorter-tenure profile — typically 1–4 years, so prepayment penalty exposure (where it exists) is concentrated in the early months.
  • NBFC-heavy distribution channel — a large share of two-wheeler financing runs through NBFCs, so the NBFC layer + fair-practice code is the more relevant control surface here.
  • Disclosure parity — same KFS and sanction-letter disclosure requirement applies regardless of loan size.

A lightweight complaint-pattern model trained on two-wheeler loan grievance text, used to cluster recurring root causes by NBFC partner — feeding a quarterly governance scorecard instead of a manual tagging exercise.

Income tax — salaried & business

Section 87A rebate

New regime: taxable income up to ₹12 lakh → zero tax (rebate up to ₹60,000). Old regime: rebate only up to ₹5 lakh income (₹12,500 max).

Standard deduction

New regime: ₹75,000 for salaried/pensioners — pushes the effective tax-free ceiling to ~₹12.75 lakh. Old regime: ₹50,000.

80C / 80D (old regime only)

80C: ₹1.5 lakh (PPF, ELSS, life insurance, tuition fees, 5-yr tax-saver FD). 80D: ₹25,000 self+family, +₹25,000 parents (<60), or +₹50,000 if parents are senior citizens.

For business income / self-employed

What changes if you're not on a salary

Regime switching is restricted. Salaried taxpayers can switch between old/new every year. If you have business or professional income, you can switch out of the new regime only once in your lifetime via Form 10-IEA, and switching back is allowed only once more — plan the choice carefully rather than year-to-year.

HRA, LTA not applicable the same way — business income doesn't carry salary-linked exemptions; deductions instead come from allowable business expenses, depreciation, and Chapter VI-A items if on the old regime.

Presumptive taxation (Sec 44AD/44ADA) remains available for eligible small businesses/professionals regardless of regime choice, taxing a deemed percentage of turnover rather than actual profit — worth checking if turnover is below the threshold.

GST registration is a separate, parallel obligation once turnover crosses ₹20–40 lakh (services/goods, threshold varies by state) — distinct from income tax regime choice.

Methodology

How this was built

Research approach

Every regulatory domain was checked against RBI Master Directions, RBI policy statements, and IRDAI circulars current to 28 June 2026, cross-referenced with regulatory press coverage to confirm effective dates. Where a rule is still in draft (e.g. the one-click sales ban), that is marked explicitly rather than presented as final. Tax slabs reflect FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27), unchanged by Budget 2026 into FY 2026-27. Eligibility/salary-slab figures are illustrative lender-comfort bands compiled from publicly available lending guidance, not a specific bank's actual policy.

Why this exists

Built as a free, open reference for anyone navigating Indian BFSI rules — students, analysts, first-time borrowers — alongside a working point of view on where a Gen AI layer fits inside an existing compliance control. The goal is a control that catches issues earlier and reports on itself, not a control that gets replaced.
